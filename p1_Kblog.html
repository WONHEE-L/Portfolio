About S.Korea

Korean Peninsula is "located between China and Japan" in geological location and sharing a land with N.Korea. Korea has distintive four seasons; spring. summer, autumn, winter. In politic, S.Korea is a constitutional democracy and use Hangul which is created in 1446 by King Sejong. Korean culture is influenced by Confucianism which supports group harmony, respect for elders, the importance of family and ancestor, also tradition.

Museums

The National Museum Of Korea

It is displayed Korean history and cultural art as a flagship museum and is located in Yongsan district, Seoul. "It is one of the most visited museum in the world and Asia and in S.Korea." The museum has been commited to various researches, exhibitions and education programs. Visiting the museum is one of favorite activities in Seoul. * Visit for more info: The National Museum of Korea

National Folk Museum Of Korea

It is "illustrated the history of traditional life of Korean people" by using immitation objects and located in Gyeongbokgung Palace, Seoul. It features materials of everyday life in Korea from prehistoric times to the end of the Joseon Dynasty. Recently, modern and contemporary items are exhibited in addition. * Visit for more info: National Folk Museum of Korea

Jindo Sea Parting

Celebrate Moses miracle in South Korea! It is natural phenomenon, the Jindo sea parting festiveal is held annually as for one time event, however the sea opens up two to three times a year between early spring and June. The sea parting connects from the mainland of jindo to small island Modo, is estimated 2.8km long and 40m wide and last for around one hour as a land bridge connecting two island. What a wonder it is!

Korean Food

KIMCHI

Kimchi is a good source of vitamins and minerals that is made of napa cabbage, main spices such as garlic, ginger, hot peppers, green onion, and fermented anchovy or shrimp for rich flavor. There are varieties of kimch based on provinces and ingredients. It is servced as a side dish, cooked with varieties of ingredients, and applied in various cooking methods. Originally, kimch is a fermented vegetable dishes.

RECIPE
BIBIMBAP

The concept is that mix rice with namul (sauteed or seasoned vegetables) and gochujang sauce, just like salad which is served vegetable with dressing and other topping ingredients. But the difference is bibimbap is a hot dish and it is a nutrious and delicious food. The common toppings are cooked meat, fried egg, seed sprouts, fish eggs,..etc. Jeonju bibimbap is the most popular one in south korea.

RECIPE
BULGOGI

Bulgogi is a korean style grilled beef dish over charcoal fire and one of popular dish in S.Korea. Commonly, it is marinated in soy dressing before cooking and stir fried with mushrooms in a pan in home cooking. It tastes mild sweet, savory, juicy and tender beef. It is served with rice and side dishes, also, add lettuce and ssamjang it can wrap bulgogi in a lettuce.

RECIPE
MULNANGMYEON

The buckwheat thin noodles are served in slushy broth, topping with thin sliced raddish or cucumber, half cut of boiled egg, sliced meat, additionally seasoned with musstard and venegar by personal flavor. People enjoy Mul Nangmyeon as lunch menu, especially during hot summer to cool down body temperature. There is another option, Bibim Naegmyeon contains similar ingredients, but with hot chilli sauce instead of icy broth.

RECIPE
TRADITIONAL BAPSANG

It means a table at which meals are included cooked rice, soup, side dishes, Jang. Side dish is comprised of kimchi, namul, vegetable dishe, fish or meat. Jang is fermented or salted foods. This is basic babsang in everyday korean diet. A variety of babsang is crafted based on provinces, ingredients, and cooking methods. Main cooking methhod is by saute, steam, boiling and grill.
